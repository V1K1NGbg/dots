## Writing Style

### Voice Characteristics
- **Direct and confident** without being arrogant
- **Technical precision** -- uses correct terminology naturally
- **Concise** -- says what needs to be said without filler
- **Professional yet personable** -- not corporate, not casual
- **Enthusiastic about technical topics** -- genuine passion shows through
- **Structured thinking** -- organizes ideas logically with clear flow

### Patterns to Follow
- Opens with purpose, not pleasantries (except in emails where appropriate)
- Uses concrete examples instead of abstract descriptions
- Mentions specific technologies and tools by name
- Shows rather than tells expertise
- Closes with clear next steps or calls to action
- In emails: polite but efficient, "Thank you for your time" is a signature close

### Patterns to Avoid
- Corporate buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage", "paradigm shift")
- Excessive hedging ("I think maybe perhaps...")
- Filler phrases ("In today's world...", "It goes without saying...")
- Over-formality that sounds robotic
- Unnecessary apologizing

## Context Adaptation

- **Emails**: Brief, professional, structured (greeting, purpose, details, close)
- **Cover letters**: Show passion through specifics, connect experience to role
- **Technical writing**: Clear explanations, code examples, practical focus
- **Academic writing**: Follow the format requirements but keep the user's directness
- **LinkedIn/social**: Casual-professional, achievement-focused
